The Mask of Materialism

A 1-minute microstory inspired by Bernardo Kastrup’s argument that historical materialism was a strategy, not a final truth.

Full Microstory

Imagine a group of early scientists in dark cloaks, huddled in secret. They whisper: “If we say the mind is just the brain, the Church can’t burn us.” That’s the mask of materialism—not truth, but protection.

Later, Diderot winks: “It doesn’t explain consciousness… but it’s a sharp sword against priests. Let’s use it.” The sword cuts the clergy’s power and hands control to new elites. Science flourishes, experiments feel “objective,” and people sigh in relief: “If brain = mind, then death = nothing. No punishment afterlife.”

Centuries pass. The mask slips. The sword grows dull. Materialism feels like an old costume—useful once, no longer fitting. Time to drop the weapon and look again.

Memory hook: Mask (protection), Sword (weapon), Relief (fear of death), Old Costume (outdated fiction).

Source context: Bernardo Kastrup, “Materialism as a political weapon” via IAI. Consider exploring idealist alternatives that treat consciousness as primary.
